Babbling Brook

Code Explanation

For this project, I was aiming to recreate a dial tone sound, which would be used to show the caller that they can dial. All values and methods were taken from the book. I started by combining 2 sine wave oscillators of 350Hz and 440Hz, which are standard for dial tones as mentioned in the book. Having these two sine waves stacked also creates the dial tone through additive synthesis. I then connected the oscillators to a gain node with a value of 0.125, which was taken from the description in the book but also ensures that the stacking of the oscillators stays at a reasonable volume. After, I connected them to a bandpass filter with a frequency of 2000, and connected that filter to a highpass filter with a frequency of 400. The connection of these filters helped to create the dial tone sound, as they distorted and thinned the audio into the standard dial tone sound.
